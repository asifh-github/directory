Job Description

Primary Function of Position:

The intern will report to Bill Nowlin, and day-to-day guidance will be delegated to either Robert Mostellar or Jeffrey Voien. Both Robert and Jeffrey report to me. Primary responsibility will be to shadow my team as they interact with business leaders to iteratively develop questions those leaders have about their business operations and their data, and develop insights iteratively. We also expect the intern to contribute meaningful analysis to, or solve, a stand-alone problem during his/her time with us.

Roles & Responsibilities:

  • Become familiar with some ISI business and/or system-generated data, their restriction, and applicable use
  • Wrangle said data into a form suitable for analysis and modeling
  • Use advanced data science techniques and Tableau visualization to prepare explorable insights for business problems posed by senior leadership
  • Join in explanatory and exploratory conversations with senior business leaders
  • Present the intern’s findings (PowerPoint) him/herself to senior leadership
    • (Unlike many data science positions where the individual interacts primarily with other data scientists, this person will interact directly with business leaders. This is a distinguishing feature of our positions that I believe should be strongly attractive to candidates.)

Qualifications

University Hiring Program Eligibility Requirements :

  • University Enrollment: Must be currently enrolled in and returning to an accredited degree-seeking academic program in the Fall.
  • Internship Work Period: Must be available to work full-time (approximately 40 hours per week) during a 10-12 week period starting May or June. Specific start dates are shared during the recruiting process.

Skills, Experience, Education, & Training:

  • Current enrollment in a Data Science degree-seeking program at the Master’s level.
  • Associated advanced data wrangling skills, including SQL and python (or equivalent, such as R) and natural language procession.
  • Associated modeling skills, including but not limited to, feature selection, recognizing and correcting bias in data, multivariate regression and logistic regression, random forest, clustering, etc. These skills should be part of any competent Master’s program.
  • Strong interpersonal skills and communication skills (verbal, written, and graphically via Tableau or similar).
