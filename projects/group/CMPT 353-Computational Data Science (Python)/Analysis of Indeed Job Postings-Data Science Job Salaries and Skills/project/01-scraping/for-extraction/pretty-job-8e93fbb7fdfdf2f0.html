About the Role:

We are looking for a highly motivated and analytically creative Associate Data Scientist to help shape the data science and advanced analytics capabilities at Dyson in the Americas.

As a member of the team, you will work directly with the commercial and operational teams to help design data science models to optimize decision making. Supported by the Americas Data Science and Analytics Engineering team, you will leverage a wide selection of data sources and tools to acquire, analyze, model, and visualize data effectively.

  • You will build predictive models using the myriad data sources available at Dyson, and enriching these with the creative sourcing of external data.
  • You will apply logical thinking and statistical learning techniques to obtain robust results the business can rely on for critical decisions.
  • You will bring Dyson’s physical retail strategy to life using advanced geo-spatial analytics
  • You will effectively communicate complex results in an easily digestible manor, focusing on action and using tools such as Microsoft PowerPoint, Jupyter Notebooks, Tableau, and custom applications.
  • You will engage with the business and data product managers to shape and refine their questions.
  • You will team up with local Data Science team and Group Data Function to get advice and support on accessing data and putting models into production.
  • Cutting edge approaches are key to keeping pace with Dyson’s innovative culture: you will be expected to stay abreast of industry trends and emerging technologies and methodologies

About You:

Person Specification / Core Competencies

  • A drive and know-how to deliver tangible business value through data science models
  • Scientific approach to solve practical problems using logical thinking.
  • Hands-on experience with leveraging data from a wide selection of data sources from different technologies e.g. BigQuery.
  • A keen understanding of data models and ETL processes. Using primarily SQL and Python and supplementing with Tableau or Looker where necessary.
  • Passion to understand the business, frame problems, and deliver practical solutions in short timeframes.
  • Ability to interact and collaborate with local analytics teams, along with Group Data Function, other market analytics teams, and the wider business.
  • Self-driven individual with drive to solve customer problems. Team player and good communicator - capable of building engaging narratives out of sparse data.
  • Good presentation and communication skills, with the ability to explain complex analytical concepts to business audiences.

Essential:

  • 1-2 years of experience supporting data science activities including data wrangling, modeling, and communicating findings in a commercial setting.
  • Strong SQL skills and comfort querying modern cloud databases such as BigQuery, Snowflake, or Redshift.
  • Geospatial data management and analytics experience using GIS tools such as Esri, QGIS, Geopandas, or BigQuery GIS or a strong desire to learn.
  • Strong Python skills with a focus on statistical and ML packages, e.g. Pandas, Scikit-Learn, Tensorflow, Keras, PyTorch, XGBoost, NumPy, SciPy.
  • Strong understanding of statistical modelling. Working experience of using advanced machine learning techniques to solve business challenges.
  • Experience collaborating with a technical team, including use of version control tools such as Git and Bitbucket, participating in code review, and adhering and contributing to team best practices.
  • Experience with using data visualization and mapping tools such as Tableau, Looker, ArcGIS Pro, or matplotlib to communicate methodology, findings, and recommendations related to analyses and models.
  • Experience in data cleansing and blending (internally and externally) to drive richer insights.
  • A strong desire to learn about various functions within the Dyson business, learn and apply new technical techniques and tools, and contribute to the Americas Data Science and Analytics Engineering team problem solving skills and best practices.

Desirable:

  • Knowledge of how to build enterprise data science products in cloud environments
  • 1-2 years of hands-on experience in Google / AWS cloud platform or AWS.
  • Exposure to or experience with Docker and Kubernetes.
  • Familiar with Linux/Unix and shell scripting, experience in cloud computing is a plus.
  • Familiar with Linux/Unix and shell scripting, experience in cloud computing is a plus
  • Knowledge of object oriented programming concepts and their application to data science pipelines.
  • Familiarity with dbt.
