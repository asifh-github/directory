Full Job Description

Duties

As a Data Scientist within the Volcano Science Center, Cascade Volcano Observatory, some of your specific duties will include:

  • Coordinate data management and integration for the Volcano Science Center.
  • Document, manage quality, and archive image data or volcano monitoring.
  • Represent the Agency, Bureau, and Volcano Science Center as a subject-matter expert in the field of InSAR and satellite remote sensing data.
  • Publish research related to remote sensing imagery of volcanic activity in peer-reviewed journals.
  • Lead research and development of new computational tools, modeling approaches, and analysis methods for remote sensing data from volcanoes.
  • Support Program and Bureau scientists with application of remote sensing data to volcano research and monitoring.
Physical Demands:
The work is usually sedentary but may include lifting of light office objects. Occasional travel is required to training, meetings, conferences, or incidental voluntary trips to the field. Incidental and optional field work to assist with ground-based volcano monitoring may occur. This may include occasional vehicle and on-foot travel to field locations and carrying loads of up to 50 lbs in rugged environments subject to extreme weather, volcanic gases, and other hazards common at volcanoes and in remote field locations.

Work Environment:
The work is generally performed in an office environment that is adequately lighted, heated, and ventilated. Travel via airplane will be required to attend space-agency meetings and to confer with scientists at academic conferences. Some of these meetings will occur outside the USA. Some weather and volcano hazards may necessitate wearing appropriate personal protective equipment (clothing, respirators, etc.) .

Qualifications

BASIC EDUCATION REQUIREMENT : Applicants must meet A or B below to satisfy the basic education requirement for Data Scientist, all grade levels.

A. Degree: Mathematics, statistics, computer science, data science or field directly related to the position. The degree must be in a major field of study (at least at the baccalaureate level) that is appropriate for the position.

B. Combination of education and experience: Courses equivalent to a major field of study (30 semester hours) as shown in paragraph A above, plus additional education or appropriate experience.

For GS-13

In addition to the basic education requirement as stated above, applicants must meet the following to qualify for the GS-13 level:

One year of appropriate professional experience that is in or directly related to the duties of the position to be filled is qualifying if it is equivalent to at least the GS-12 level in the Federal service, and if it equipped the applicant with the knowledge, skills and abilities to perform successfully the duties of the position.

Examples of experience may include : plans, organizes, and coordinates protocols and procedures necessary for data management, data integration, and publication of data and metadata; conducts analysis and interpretation on available data and evaluates the effectiveness of programs/applications and various types of database and analytical tools (e.g., Python, Matlab, ArcGIS, SQL, etc.); modifies instructions, methodologies, or guidelines as necessary to achieve program objectives; reviews data management plans and provides advice and assistance in the development and management of data sharing agreements; develops tools for processing and analysis of SAR data applied to evaluation of volcanic activity and hazards; leads operational and scientific responses to volcanic unrest and eruption as part of a team (e.g., a volcano observatory).

You must meet all qualification and eligibility requirements for the position by the closing date of the announcement.
