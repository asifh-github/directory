Duties

At the full performance level, major duties include:

  • Manages complex, broad data analysis assignments using approaches common to the field of data science including artificial intelligence/machine learning (AI/ML) techniques, data visualizations, and other analysis supporting policy, supervision, and resolution planning related to large complex financial institutions (LCFIs) including banks, bank holding companies (BHCs) and other systemically important non-bank institutions with national or international operations.
  • Conducts analysis on and derives meaning from large and complex data sources in multiple formats including structured, unstructured, and semi-structured data using statistical software and/or programing languages.
  • Utilizes software and/or programming languages to automate existing processes. Develops utilities or scripts to programmatically deploy and update data visualizations, data sources, and other analytic tools to web based platforms. Uses appropriate analytical software and techniques to programmatically prepare data for analysis and clean imperfect data.
  • Utilizes software development lifecycle (SDLC) methodologies and tools to build efficient and repeatable AI/ML analytical tools and data pipelines.
  • Communicates statistical results and analysis, and artificial intelligence/machine learning (AI/ML) concepts orally and in writing to both technical and non-technical audiences. Presents results to internal and external groups including technical audiences and senior management.

Qualifications

Qualifying experience may be obtained in the private or public sector. Experience refers to paid and unpaid experience, including volunteer work done through National Service programs (e.g. Peace Corps, AmeriCorps) and other organizations (e.g., professional; philanthropic, religious spiritual; community; student, social). Volunteer work helps build critical competencies, knowledge, and skills and can provide valuable training and experience that translates directly to paid employment. You will receive credit for all qualifying experience, including volunteer experience. Additional qualifications information can be found here.
To qualify for the CG-13, applicants must have completed at least one year of specialized experience equivalent to at least the CG-12 grade level or above in the Federal service. Specialized experience is defined as experience conducting data analytics using techniques such as machine learning, natural language processing, robotics process automation, or artificial intelligence; AND conducting analysis using software and/or programming languages such as R, Python, or SAS to test hypotheses and predict outcomes; AND
using data tools and techniques to process large, datasets into structured formats for matching, analysis, or estimation.
To qualify for the CG-14, applicants must have completed at least one year of specialized experience equivalent to at least the CG-13 grade level or above in the Federal service. Specialized experience is defined as experience designing and developing of data analytics/science related products using techniques such as machine learning, natural language processing, robotics process automation, or artificial intelligence; AND conducting analysis and automate business processes using software and/or programming languages such as R, Python, or SAS to test hypotheses and predict outcomes; AND developing and executing machine learning models (e.g. deep neural networks, support vector machines, random forests, etc.).
Basic Requirement:
A. Degree: Mathematics, statistics, computer science, data science or field directly related to the position. The degree must be in a major field of study (at least at the baccalaureate level) that is appropriate for the position. -OR-
B. Combination of education and experience: Courses must be equivalent to a major field of study (30 semester hours) in Mathematics, statistics, computer science, data science or field directly related to the position.

How You Will Be Evaluated

You will be evaluated for this job based on how well you meet the qualifications above.

Your resume and the online assessment questionnaire will be reviewed to determine whether you meet the qualification requirements outlined in this announcement. Therefore, it is imperative that your resume contain sufficiently detailed information upon which to make the qualification. Please ensure that your resume contains specific information such as position titles, beginning and ending dates of employment for each position, average number of hours worked per week, and if the position is/was in the Federal government, you should provide the position series and grade level.

Your resume will also be evaluated to measure your responses to the assessment questions. If you rated yourself higher on the questionnaire than what is supported by your resume, your overall qualifications assessment may be adversely affected.

If you are found qualified, you will be placed in one of three categories: Best Qualified, Highly Qualified, or Qualified. These category assignments are a measure of the degree in which your background and responses to the assessment questions match the competencies/knowledge, skills, and abilities (KSAs) listed below. Within these categories, candidates eligible for veterans’ preference will receive selection priority over non-veterans.

  • Knowledge in the application and interpretation of analytical techniques including supervised and unsupervised learning utilizing statistical programs and programming languages (such as Python, R, SAS, etc.).
  • Knowledge of economics, finance, statistics, and econometric modeling and quantitative financial analysis.
  • Ability to utilize data visualization tools (such as R, Python, D3, Tableau) to communicate and display analytical results including the development of graphics, charts, and complex visualizations. Experience designing visualizations tailored to specific audiences (i.e., analysts, examination staff, and senior management).
  • Ability to develop and implement automated solutions to complex analytical problems including identification and on-boarding of relevant data sources; data cleansing, manipulation, and creation of derived metrics; identification and implementation of appropriate AI/ML methodologies; and visualization and communication of analytical results.
  • Knowledge in working with tools and techniques for analyzing large and complex datasets (including structured and unstructured data) in distributed or HPC environments (i.e. Spark, Greenplum, etc.)
  • Skill in communication and negotiation techniques to establish and maintain effective working relationships within the Agency and Division, as well as other federal regulators, government agencies, and private sector organizations.
  • Ability to organize and analyze information and produce authoritative reports, briefings, and presentations.
  • Ability to advocate or defend a position to senior leadership and/or other subject matter experts.

You do not need to respond separately to these KSAs. Your answers to the online questionnaire and resume will serve as responses to the KSAs.
