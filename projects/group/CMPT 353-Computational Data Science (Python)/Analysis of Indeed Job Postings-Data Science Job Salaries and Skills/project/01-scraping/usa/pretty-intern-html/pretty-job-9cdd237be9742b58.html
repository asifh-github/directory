About Allogene Therapeutics, Inc:

Allogene Therapeutics, with headquarters in South San Francisco, is a clinical-stage biotechnology company pioneering the development of allogeneic chimeric antigen receptor T cell (AlloCAR T™) therapies for cancer. Led by a management team with significant experience in cell therapy, Allogene is developing a pipeline of “off-the-shelf” CAR T cell therapy candidates with the goal of delivering readily available cell therapy on-demand, more reliably, and at greater scale to more patients. For more information, please visit www.allogene.com, and follow @AllogeneTx on Twitter and LinkedIn.


About the role:

Unique opportunity in our Data and Analytics space to utilize your science background and learn some new skills in data analytics. This position will contribute to the development and use of data lake and work in close collaboration with key stakeholders in the Research, Process Development, and Biometrics teams. Contribute to various project activities: understanding and capturing analytics requirements, solutioning, testing the solutions, documentation, communication, and training. This position is reporting to the Data Analytics team in Information Technology.

Responsibilities include, but are not limited to:

  • Contribute towards identifying opportunities for consolidating, integrating, standardizing data and visualizations
  • Capture and document requirements (problem statements and desired solutions)
  • Analyze and build data models and visualizations
  • Showcase the data analytics capabilities and help drive data culture within the R&D teams
  • Explore options and present recommendations
  • Testing the solutions
  • Creating presentations for core team and management
  • Managing project activities and timelines
  • Prioritizing requirements/activities

Position Requirements & Experience:

  • Major in Molecular Biology, Cell Biology, Immunology, Bio Engineering, Biometrics, Biochemistry, Data Science, or related field required (or relevant experience)
  • Must be a sophomore or junior in a US University
  • A keen interest in data analytics/data science/statistics
  • Excellent organizational skills and an ability to prioritize effectively to deliver results within reasonably established timelines.
  • Ability to work independently and as part of a team.
  • Strong interpersonal skills including verbal and written communication are essential in this collaborative work environment.
  • Candidates must be authorized to work in the U.S.
