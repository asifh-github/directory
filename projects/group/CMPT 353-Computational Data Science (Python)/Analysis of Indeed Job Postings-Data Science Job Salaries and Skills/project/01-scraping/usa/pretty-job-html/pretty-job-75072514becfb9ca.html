Clinical Data Scientist

Remote position in USA

10-12 months contract position

Invitae is dedicated to bringing comprehensive genetic information into mainstream medicine to improve healthcare for billions of people! Our team is driven to make a difference for the patients we serve. We are leading the transformation of the genetics industry, by making clinical-grade genetic information affordable and accessible to guide health decisions across all stages of life.

We are looking for a data scientist with domain knowledge in healthcare to join our AI Computational Biology team. Our team includes computational biologists, computer scientists, and engineers working together to enhance and demonstrate the value of clinico-genomics data for precision medicine. In this contract position, you will work closely with an external partner to generate insights from clinical data to benefit patients and bring a better understanding of their disease.

What you'll do:

  • Responsible for the analysis of structured medical records to answer client inquiries regarding a cohort of blood cancer patients
  • Work with clinical scientists internally and externally to define questions of interest and map approaches to answer them
  • Query a database of structured patient medical records to describe variables and interactions between them
  • Develop and apply statistical models to identify associations between concepts such as treatment trajectories and healthcare outcomes, understand causal relationships, and predict clinical variables
  • Cooperate with other members of the AI Computational Biology team to generalize solutions, so that can be applied to similar problems in other clinical areas
  • Prepare reports, slide decks, presentations and present to clients

What you bring:

  • Master's or equivalent experience in biostatistics, computational biology, computer science, or in a related field
  • Strength in core statistical principles and methods such as data imputation, hypothesis testing, and regression modeling, and experience with prediction modeling
  • Knowledge of SQL and relational databases
  • Expert in Python 3 and common libraries (e.g. numpy, scipy, pandas) and data visualization
  • Experience working in a production development environment using version control
  • Excellent documentation practices, and presentation skills to non-technical audiences

Preferred skills:

  • Prior experience working with electronic medical records and familiarity with clinical data standards (ICD9/10, SNOMED, LOINC, FHIR, etc.)
  • Oncology experience preferred, hematological malignancies a plus.

Please apply even if you don't meet all of the "What you bring" requirements noted. It's rare that someone checks every single item, it's ok, we encourage you to apply anyways.
