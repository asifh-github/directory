Position Highlights
The Data Scientist III will design and manage data collection systems for multiple ongoing human subjects research studies, ensuring data integrity and compliance with regulatory requirements. They will also develop and implement data-driven operational support systems for a growing research team. This position will conduct analyses from multiple data sources for grant submissions, publication, and daily research operations. They will be responsible for overseeing data entry and monitoring activities of all research team members.

Outstanding UA benefits include health, dental, and vision insurance plans; life insurance and disability programs; paid vacation, sick leave, and holidays; UA/ASU/NAU tuition reduction for the employee and qualified family members; state and optional retirement plans; access to UA recreation and cultural activities; and more!

The University of Arizona has been recognized for our innovative work-life programs.



Duties & Responsibilities
  • Developing and maintaining databases for research and operational support.
  • Designing, implementing, and coordinating data collection activities and process improvements.
  • Analyzing and reporting data from various databases and sources for internal operations, grant application and publications.
  • Miscellaneous duties as needed.

Skills, Abilities, Knowledge:

  • Significant attention to detail with a high-level of accurate and effective follow-through.
  • Motivated to contribute to the growth of an exciting research team and program.
  • Knowledge and application of the principles, practices and techniques of clinical research.
  • Strong verbal and written communication skills.
  • Ability to work both independently and with a team, resolving issues with a positive outcome.
  • High-level of integrity, ethics and professionalism.
  • Proficiency in R or Python.
  • One (1)+ years experience with REDCap database design and management.

Minimum Qualifications
  • Master's degree required.
  • Five (5) years of relevant work experience is required.

Preferred Qualifications
  • Experience working on projects related to addiction, substance abuse disorders, and/or women's health.

  • Experience working at the University of Arizona.

  • Experience with peer reviewed publications.

  • Supervisory experience.
