Full Job Description

This role requires proof of COVID-19 vaccination or an approved exemption as a condition of employment.

The Data Analytics Internship will be a full-time (40 hours per week) opportunity for 12 weeks starting in May and ending in August. This is a great opportunity to gain professional experience by working closely with the data analytics team on various data-related activities that support critical decision-making for our company.


As part of the internship program, students will gain additional skills to prepare them for entering the job market, including interviewing, presentations, and a performance evaluation to further enhance their experience.


Experience is Everything;

At LCS, experience is everything. We provide you the opportunity to use your talents in a progressive, growing organization that makes a positive difference in the lives of the seniors we serve. If you are seeking an organization that gives back, you’ll love working here. Our principles and hospitality promises define our company culture. LCS employees can be found participating in volunteer activities, getting involved in our committees or collaborating with team members in our innovative work space. The Role: In this role you’ll help us continue to activate our analytics strategy by working on key business problems that deliver results and delight our residents. You’ll gain hands on experience working with our cloud analytics platform to build a variety of analytical solutions such as dashboards, models, causal inference and more. You will build technical skills using SQL, Python and Power BI while also getting the opportunity to communicate your findings to internal business partners. This role will provide the opportunity to partner closely with other analytics roles such as data engineering, data science, data analysis and data governance.


Experience:

  • Minimum of 2 years completed towards bachelor’s degree in data science, business analytics, statistics, operation research, computer science, or related field

Knowledge & Skills:

  • Experience with R or Python and familiarity with SQL
  • Exposure to statistical concepts such as machine learning or bayesian statistics
  • Curiosity and drive to dig deep into disparate data sources
  • Strong technical skills with the ability to learn new tools quickly
  • Comfortable communicating technical results to a non-technical audience
  • Must be highly motivated and able to work full-time during the summer of 2023 in the Des Moines corporate office
