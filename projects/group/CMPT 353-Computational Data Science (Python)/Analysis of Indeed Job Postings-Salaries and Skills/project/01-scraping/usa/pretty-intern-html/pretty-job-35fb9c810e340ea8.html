Essential Duties and Responsibilities:
The Advanced Analytics team is looking for enthusiastic data science interns to lead the design, development, and deployment of advanced analytics solutions supporting CGI’s strategic business priorities.
The Data Science Intern will initiate, manage, and deliver a project, including creating and training statistical models and algorithms and providing insights and user-friendly tools and capabilities to enable data-driven decisions by business stakeholders. As a member of the Advanced Analytics team, you will work closely with senior data scientists, engineers, architects, and business partners.
  • Collaborate with stakeholders and advanced analytics business partners to understand business needs and to identify and define advanced analytics solutions
  • Evaluate analytical and machine learning techniques to achieve business objectives
  • Execute analytic activities supporting solution development, including data gathering, cleansing, and integration, statistical transformation and modeling, and feature engineering and selection
  • Design, develop, and deploy advanced analytics solution, including predictive and prescriptive outputs, analytical tools and visualization capabilities to enable business users to make data-driven decisions
  • Create documentation and training materials for end-user understanding
  • Effectively communicate results and findings to stakeholders and Chamberlain’s Executive Leadership Team upon internship completion
Minimum Qualifications:
  • Currently pursuing a Bachelor’s or Master’s Degree in Statistics, Data Science, Business Analytics, Computer Science, or other quantitative field of study
  • Must be committed to the duration of the Program Semester
    • Must possess an unrestricted right to work in the United States
  • Must be local as 3 days are required to be in the Oak Brook office
  • GPA minimum: 3.5 on a 4.0 point scale
  • Currently, not hiring individuals for Intern/Co-Op roles who currently (or in the future) require sponsorship for employment, including candidates with OPT authorization.
Knowledge, Skills, and Abilities:
  • Experience using Python and/or R to manipulate and draw insights from data
  • Knowledge of advanced analytical techniques, including multivariate statistics, forecasting, predictive modeling, and cluster analysis
  • Familiarity with data visualization tools (e.g., Power BI)
  • Problem solving and analytical thinking and skills
  • Teamwork and collaboration
  • Strong communication and organizational skills
  • Proficient in Microsoft Office
