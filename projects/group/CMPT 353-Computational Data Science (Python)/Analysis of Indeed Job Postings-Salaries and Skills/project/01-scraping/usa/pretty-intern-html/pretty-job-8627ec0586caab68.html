Link Logistics Real Estate (“Link”) owns and operates the largest U.S.-only portfolio of logistics real estate, leasing warehouse space to everyone from e-commerce giants to mom-and-pop shops. Established by Blackstone in 2019, we combine scale, cutting-edge technology and logistics expertise to help our customers grow their businesses. Our portfolio includes more than 500 million square feet in dynamic markets nationwide. About 5 percent of U.S. GDP—translating to some $1 trillion every year—flows through the space Link customers occupy in our facilities.

Because we believe that good business must be synonymous with doing good, strong environmental, social and governance practices are foundational to our identity as a firm. These practices include setting ambitious goals to combat climate change, partnering with local nonprofits, and prioritizing internal diversity, equity and inclusion efforts. We seek to use our position, ideas, and influence to drive progress in our industry and the wider world.

In this role, Data Science Intern , Research & Analytics , you will be responsible for supporting the team’s efforts to build and implement machine learning models to inform better business decision making. You will be working with a cross-functional team of both supply chain and real estate veterans while developing a data science skillset via in-depth projects that’ll involve applying statistical and technical rigor to solve key challenges surrounding the world of industrial real estate.
RESPONSIBILITIES:
What You Will Bring:
  • Collect, clean, and analyze raw and complex industry data from disparate sources to identify patterns and statistically significant signals
  • Research and apply various modeling and analytical techniques to build solutions that answer key business needs and goals
  • Interpret models and articulate findings into concrete measurable actions that will inform Link’s investment, operational, and strategic direction
  • Provider broader analytical support to the Research & Analytics team
QUALIFICATIONS:
  • Currently pursuing an undergraduate or graduate degree with an expected graduation of Winter 2023 or Spring 2024
  • Proficiency in Excel and Python (or R)
  • Relevant experience in statistics, economics, computer science, or similar quantitative discipline
