As our NIKE Sport Research Lab (NSRL) Data Science Intern, you will complete research projects aimed at making athletes* measurably better with data-powered products and innovations. You will have the opportunity to work closely with game changers in biomechanics, physiology and behavior to develop metrics, insights and predictive models to improve performance. Projects will involve using diverse, large and complex athlete data in a cloud environment, and building compelling data visualizations for diverse audiences.

Qualifications

  • Pursuing an advanced degree (Master’s or PhD) in a quantitative field (Engineering, Computer Science, Data Science, Human Research or Sport Science, etc.) with an expected graduation of Winter 2023 or Spring 2024

  • Demonstrated projects in programming language (e.g. Python, C++) and ML/DL frameworks (e.g. PyTorch, TensorFlow, Keras, Scikit-learn) including data synthesis, machine learning, statistical modeling or signal processing

  • Demonstrated experience with data visualization and interaction tools and frameworks (e.g. Tableau, Plotly, Streamlit, Matplotlib, d3.js)

  • Ability to read, interpret, and implement methods described in research papers, journals, and articles in signal processing, machine/deep learning, behavioral economics and related fields

  • Ability to build software tools or applications and dashboards using front-end development skills

  • Proven experience communicating technical information in written and verbal formats

  • Proven ability to sift through data, identify critical information, develop hypotheses, perform thorough analyses, and make recommendations to broader audiences

  • Strong interpersonal skills and open to learning new tools/techniques

  • Passionate about sport, fitness, exercise science and related areas

Preferred Qualifications

  • 2+ years of experience in a role developing predictive or explanatory models and/or experimentation processes

  • Experience with neural nets and deep architectures

  • Experience with distributed computing systems and environments (Spark, Hive, Airflow, Kubernetes etc)

  • Experience with moderate to large-scale data sets (>100GB)

  • Experience in at least one production-quality programming language (e.g. java, python, scala, C++)

  • Experience with activity recognition/classification, physiological signal processing, time-series analysis, biomechanical modeling or related areas using data captured in real-world settings
