We are seeking well-qualified individuals for variety of internships in our Enforcement Department beginning next Summer.
Essential Job Functions:
The Enforcement Data & Analytics Intern will work with FINRA’s investigation teams to gain hands-on experience in projects that may include:
  • Gain an understanding and working knowledge of FINRA's role and responsibilities in the securities industry
  • Develop an understanding of investigation approaches, tools, and methodologies.
  • Assist investigators and attorneys with the investigative process, including analysis of market data, trade data and other evidence obtained in connection with Enforcement investigations
  • Accurately perform data analysis under supervision with consistent methodology
  • Effectively describe data analysis and findings, noting exceptions or assumptions embedded in analyses
  • Participate in project discussion meetings and provide status updates, as appropriate.
Education/Experience Requirements:
  • Enrollment in an undergraduate program in Finance, Accounting, Statistics, and Data Science or related disciplines; or the first year of a Masters program in Finance, Accounting, Statistics, and Data Science or related disciplines
  • Work experience in a financial, brokerage or investment environment is preferred.
  • Hands-on development experience with SQL, Python, PySpark, R, etc.
  • Demonstrated ability to learn and develop proficiency in new concepts/techniques and apply knowledge
  • Strong work ethic, positive attitudes, and professional demeanor.

Important Information
FINRA’s Code of Conduct imposes restrictions on employees’ investments and requires financial disclosures that are uniquely related to our role as a securities regulator. FINRA employees are required to disclose to FINRA all brokerage accounts that they maintain, and those in which they control trading or have a financial interest (including any trust account of which they are a trustee or beneficiary and all accounts of a spouse, domestic partner or minor child who lives with the employee) and to authorize their broker-dealers to provide FINRA with duplicate statements for all of those accounts. All of those accounts are subject to the Code’s investment and securities account restrictions, and new employees must comply with those investment restrictions—including disposing of any security issued by a company on FINRA’s Prohibited Company List or obtaining a written waiver from their Executive Vice President—by the date they begin employment with FINRA. Employees may only maintain securities accounts that must be disclosed to FINRA at one or more securities firms that provide an electronic feed (e-feed) of data to FINRA, and must move securities accounts from other securities firms to a firm that provides an e-feed within three months of beginning employment.

As standard practice, employees must also execute FINRA’s Employee Confidentiality and Invention Assignment Agreement without qualification or modification and comply with the company’s policy on nepotism.
