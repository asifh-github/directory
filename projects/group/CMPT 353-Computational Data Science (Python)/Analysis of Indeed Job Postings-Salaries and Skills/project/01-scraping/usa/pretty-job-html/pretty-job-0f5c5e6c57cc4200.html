Full Job Description

Mather Economics seeks new graduates to early-stage career individuals who have strong technical skills with the ability to write efficient and maintainable code. Candidates should have experience in data cleaning, manipulation and management through course work, fellowships, independent research, internships, and/or previous work experience. Individuals should be detail-oriented, organized and have a good understanding of econometric modeling and coding.
Candidates should have a four-year degree from an accredited college or university with a degree in economics, computer science, mathematics, or a related field. Degrees should be accompanied with relevant internship or work experience. Technical skills should include Stata, SQL, and Python.
We are seeking individuals who stress they want a fast-paced, team focused atmosphere, have a desire to continually learn and improve technical skills, and show a desire to grow into future positions with the company.

Responsibilities included but are not limited to:

  • Perform data management such as data validation, quality assurance and health reporting
  • Handle data operational tasks, such as running, modifying and automating recurring processes using SQL and Python
  • Apply econometric modeling coefficients to client datasets using STATA
  • Pull and manipulate data for client requested reporting
  • Write queries to pull output for A/B testing results to be used in client reporting
  • Maintain data quality assurance on a regular basis for data feeds from clients and to clients
  • Problem-solve data or process issues
  • Create and updating health reporting to check for data quality assurance, anomalies and process success
  • Communicate with consulting team on operational tasks and timelines
  • Communicate with team on key performance insights (KPI) on health reporting and quality acceptance thresholds
  • Adjust recurring processes and data to account for any errors outside of quality acceptance thresholds

Candidates with 1-2 years of previous work experience will be considered for the Analyst II position.
This position works out of our headquarters in the Sandy Springs area of Atlanta. This is a full-time salaried position with full health benefits, 401K, paid vacation, and an annual bonus.
