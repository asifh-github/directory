WHO WE ARE

The Metropolitan Council’s Community Development Division is accepting applications for a Data Visualization Specialist.

This position is eligible for a hybrid (both remote and onsite) telework arrangement. The candidate's permanent residence must be in Minnesota or Wisconsin.

We are the Metropolitan Council, the regional government for the seven-county Twin Cities metropolitan area. We plan 30 years ahead for the future of the metropolitan area and provide regional transportation, wastewater, and housing services. More information about us is on our website.

Metropolitan Council’s Community Development division is responsible for the Council's regional long-range planning efforts; planning and technical assistance to local governments; planning for and funding the regional parks and open space system; delivering rent assistance through the Metropolitan Housing and Redevelopment Authority; and delivering the Livable Communities Act grant programs.

We are committed to hiring and supporting a diverse workforce that reflects the communities we serve.

In Community Development, we have set the following goals to guide our work:


  • Our division builds competency, takes meaningful actions, and creates the necessary infrastructure to advance racial equity.
  • Our division’s workplace culture is diverse, inclusive, and empowers all employees.
The C ommunity Development Research team collects, disseminates, and analyzes data about demographic trends, development patterns, climate change, parks visitation, equity, and the regional economy. We believe research can improve the lives of residents in our region, and see our skills, creativity, and platforms as resources to further shared understanding about regional issues that matter.

This position is part of the strategy and product design group , a unit within the community development research team. We are creative problem-solvers who think how research products are developed is as important as the product itself. We center people, we integrate equity, and we transform technical into accessible through—and by—design.

How your work would contribute to our organization and the Twin Cities region:

The Data Visualization Specialist will join a collaborative team from various data science, sociology, geography/GIS, economics, and related backgrounds who value people, curiosity, humility, and justice.

What you would do in this job

Collaborate with researchers and data scientists to create digital research content


  • Build public-facing interactives such as dashboards, maps, dynamic tables, and digital reports using Tableau, ESRI ArcOnline, and other software/tools
  • Support research product development from requirements gathering and iterative prototyping to refinements based on user requirements and ongoing web analytics
  • Work with both source data and analyzed data/outputs to create interactives; use version control and other documentation to ensure accuracy, replicability, and clarity with collaborators
Use and grow your data visualization and design skills to enhance digital research content
  • Use and expand your knowledge of data visualization, design, usability, and web accessibility best practices and share this information with research staff and other stakeholders
  • Engage in virtual quantitative and qualitative user experience (UX) research efforts to improve and refine research products
  • Present approaches and results to interagency practitioners groups, conferences, and other forums
Contribute to the community development research team’s strategic priorities
  • Contribute ideas to our emerging data visualization best practices and innovative uses of current tools/software
  • Support the creation and implementation of the research team’s 2023 digital content strategy
  • Build deeper, ongoing relationships with current and potential audiences within and outside the organization
  • Play a key role in the reimagining of our current web pages and flagship data tools like Community Profiles

What education and experience are required for this job (minimum qualifications)

A Master's degree in data science, data analytics and visualization, business analytics, data journalism, information design, science communications, social sciences, or a related field AND a minimum of one (1) year of experience working with data to produce effective interactive data visualizations, web applications, and/or information graphics


OR

A Bachelor's degree in data science, applied data analytics, computer science, human-computer interaction, journalism, social sciences, or a related field AND a minimum of three (3) years of experience working with data to produce effective interactive data visualizations, web applications, and/or information graphics
OR

An A ssociate's degree in data science, data analytics, data visualization, applied science, or a related field AND a minimum of five (5) years of experience working with data to produce effective interactive data visualizations, web applications, and/or information graphics
OR

A high school diploma/GED AND a minimum of seven (7) years of experience working with data to produce effective interactive data visualizations, web applications, and/or information graphics

What additional skills and experience would be helpful in this job (desired qualifications ):
  • A solid foundation of statistics, data cleaning, and data analysis; you must be comfortable working with data using Microsoft Excel, Tableau, R, or equivalent statistical applications or business intelligence tools
  • Experience building interactive data visualizations like dashboards, charts, and/or maps with Tableau (strongly preferred) or equivalent business intelligence tools. Programming languages like R, Python or SQL are not required but helpful
  • An understanding of public sector datasets (such as census data) and spatial data; familiarity and interest in public policy topics relevant to community development
  • Experience/training—or growth capacity—to engage in conversations about race and equity and to integrate equity initiatives into your work
  • Experience receiving and responding to collaborator and user feedback; experience/training or —growth capacity—in human-centered design, design thinking, or similar user-centered mindsets and practices
  • Working knowledge of Adobe Creative Suite (specifically InDesign, Illustrator, and XD)
What knowledge, skills and abilities you should have within the first six months on the job :
  • Demonstrated knowledge of statistics, data cleaning, and data analysis; must be comfortable working with data using Microsoft Excel, Tableau, R, or equivalent statistical applications or business intelligence tools
  • Ability to learn new technologies including Microsoft Teams extensions, Mural, User Zoom, and/or Mentimeter to facilitate virtual engagement and collaboration; and Tableau Prep Builder, relational database structure, Github, and data management practices to align with shared research team data practices
  • Understands how to validate data transformations to ensure accuracy and methodological soundness of interactive tools; can interpret results and explain them to others in accessible, plain language
  • Analyzes project requirements and user inputs to effectively identify solutions and make decisions that optimize usability and accessibility
  • Uses excellent written and verbal communication skills and project management experience to prioritize assignments and meet concurrent deadlines with strong attention to detail
  • Develops and maintains positive working relationships with colleagues, collaborators, stakeholders, and research product users; demonstrates commitment to achieving research team's strategic priorities and other divisional goals
  • Documents data sources, transformations, analyses, and other decisions using the research team's shared practices; uses version control in various platforms to ensure accuracy and replicability of all research products
  • Demonstrates the ability to work both independently and collaboratively; communicates meaningful project updates, and seeks input and/or involvement from others as appropriate to move work forward
  • Adheres to internal organizational policies, practices, and procedures including data practices, ethics, and required trainings
